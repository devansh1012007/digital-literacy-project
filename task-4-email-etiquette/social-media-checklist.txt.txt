Social Media Do’s and Don’ts for College Students
The 5 Do’s (Best Practices)

Privacy Audit: Regularly check and update your account privacy settings to ensure personal information is only visible to trusted friends.

Think Before Posting: Consider if you would be comfortable with a future employer or a professor seeing your post before you hit "Share".

Fact-Check Content: Verify the authenticity of news or viral posts before sharing them to prevent the spread of misinformation.

Professional Networking: Use platforms like LinkedIn to follow industry leaders and engage in meaningful academic discussions.Respectful 

Engagement: Maintain a polite and constructive tone when commenting on others' posts, even if you disagree with their viewpoint.

The 5 Don’ts (Risks to Avoid)

Oversharing Location: Avoid posting real-time locations or travel plans, as this can compromise your physical safety.

Venting About Academics: Never post derogatory comments about your professors, institution, or peers, as these can be traced back to you.

Sharing Sensitive Data: Do not post photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or QR codes that might contain your registration details.

Engaging with Trolls: Avoid getting into heated digital arguments or "flame wars" that can damage your online reputation.

Plagiarizing Content: Do not post someone else’s creative work, photos, or research without giving proper credit or obtaining permission.
